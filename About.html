About Flickr
Flickr - almost certainly the best online photo management and sharing application in the world - has two main goals:
1. We want to help people make their photos available to the people who matter to them.
Maybe they want to keep a blog of moments captured on their camera , or maybe they want to show off their best pictures or video to the whole world in aa bid for web celebrity.Or maybe they want to securely and privately share photos off their kids with their family across the country.Flickr makes all these things possible and more!

To do this, we want to get photos and video into and out of the system in as many ways as we can: from the web, from mobile devices ,from the users home computers and from whatever software they are using to manage their content . And we want to be able to push them out in as many ways as possible:on the Flickr website,RSS feeds,by email,by posting to outside blogs or ways we haven't thought of yet.What else are we going to use those smart refrigerators for?
2. We want to enable new ways of organizing photos and video.
Once you make the switch too digital , itt is all too easy to get over whelmed with the sheer number of photos you take or videos you shoot with that itchy trigger finger. Albums, the principal way people go about organizing things today , are great until you get to 20 or 30 or 50 of them . They worked in days
of getting rolls of film developed, but the "album" metaphor is in desperate need of Florida condo and
full retirement.
Flickr continues to evolve in myriad ways, all of which are designed to make itt easier and better .
